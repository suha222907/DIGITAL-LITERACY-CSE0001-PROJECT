UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime in which criminals trick people into transferring money online. This type of fraud usually happens through fake phone calls, messages, QR codes, or payment links.

A typical fraud happens step-by-step like this: first, the scammer contacts the victim by pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or online seller. 

They create urgency by saying there is an issue with the bank account, KYC update, refund, or prize money. Next, they ask the victim to click on a fake payment link, scan a QR code, or share OTP and UPI PIN details.

Many people mistakenly think scanning a QR code will help them receive money, but instead it authorizes a payment from their account. Once the victim enters the PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

Students, senior citizens, and people who frequently use online shopping or digital payment apps are common targets because they may trust such messages quickly.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, identity misuse, and in some cases unauthorized access to bank accounts. Victims may also lose trust in digital payment systems. 

To prevent this, users should never share OTP or UPI PIN, should verify callers, and must remember that money can only be received without entering a PIN.
